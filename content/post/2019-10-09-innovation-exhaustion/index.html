  • What is innovation?

A friend accused me of getting cranky in my dotage. This piece perhaps is the digital equivalent of a get-off-my-lawn sign. You’ve been warned.

I’m tired of innovation.1

Not the idea, just the word. It’s lost meaning, I feel. It’s up there with synergy and _________ and all the other words that’ve been over-wrought, under-appreciated, had their teeth filed down and their blood squeezes from them.

When’s the last time you heard someone mention innovation, especially by or in reference to something administrative, and were actually excited to hear what it was?

It’s been a long time for me.

So let’s dig into this a bit.

What is innovation?

More accurately, what is innovation supposed to be?

Innovation, the word, feels like it’s gone the way of “tenure.” Remember when tenure was supposed to be the shield against public whim or arbitrary leadership tides? And somehow it’s become something different? Yeah. It’s like that.
